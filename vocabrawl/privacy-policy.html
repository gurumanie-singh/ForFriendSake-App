Privacy Policy

Effective Date: [EFFECTIVE DATE]

Vocabrawl is a word game for iPhone. We built it to be private by design. This page explains, in plain English, exactly what the app does and does not do with your information.

The short version

Vocabrawl does not collect, store, sell, or transmit your personal data. There are no accounts to create, no emails to enter, and no trackers or advertising. Online play is handled entirely by Apple’s Game Center.

What we don’t collect

  • No name, email address, or phone number.
  • No analytics, advertising identifiers, or third-party trackers.
  • No location data.
  • No payment information — Vocabrawl is free.

Game Center

Online and turn-based matches use Apple’s Game Center. When you play online, Apple manages your player identity, matchmaking, and saved match data on its own servers under Apple’s Privacy Policy. Vocabrawl never receives or stores your Apple ID, your real name, or your email. We only see the anonymized player information Game Center provides so a match can take place, and we do not keep it after the match ends.

Data stored on your device

Vocabrawl saves a small amount of non-sensitive information locally on your device using the system’s standard preferences storage (UserDefaults), such as your chosen display name for solo play and whether you have completed the intro. This data never leaves your device and is removed if you delete the app.

No custom servers

Vocabrawl has no backend of its own. Solo games run entirely on your device. Online game state lives in Game Center (Apple’s servers), not ours.

Children’s privacy

Because Vocabrawl collects no personal data, it complies with the baseline requirements of children’s privacy regulations such as COPPA, and with the EU’s GDPR, by virtue of not processing personal data. Game Center accounts for minors are governed by Apple’s Family Sharing and parental controls.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update this page and revise the effective date above.
